DORMAA WEST DISTRICT ASSEMBLY DONATES TO PEOPLE WITH DISABILITIES (PWDs)

2022

Yesterday, Friday, 11th February, 2022, the Dormaa West District Assembly in its effort to support the government in achieving the sustainable development goal 8.5 which seeks to achieve full and productive employment for persons with disabilities held an event at the DCE's residence at Nkrankwanta (District Capital) to support some selected disabled persons in the district. The event which was organized by the department of social welfare of the Dormaa West District Assembly was graced by the District Chief Executive, Hon. Francis kwadwo Oppong, the district Coordinating Director, Mr Idrisu mahama and some heads of department at the assembly. At the event, items which included refrigerators, industrial sewing machines, provisions, hair driers, Fufu pounding machines, knapsack sprayers, motorised sprayers, cheques etc were given out to persons with disabilities in the district. In an introductory address by the head of social welfare department at the Dormaa West District Assembly, Mr. Eric Okrah reiterated on the government’s decision to improve the living standards of the persons with disability in the country. Further in his address, he said the motive for the event was a conscious effort to see to it that every person with disability in the district lives to achieve their full potential. The District Chief Executive, Hon. Francis Kwadwo Oppong in his speech outlined the government’s intentions to develop the Dormaa West District. He touched on the planting for food and jobs, one district one factory which are all initiatives by the government to improve the standards of living of the Ghanaian citizenry, and massive road construction throughout the country which the district is also benefiting from. Addressing issues on persons with disabilities, he said the government has increased the amount to be given to the disabled persons from 3% to 5% in the district assembly’s common fund. He also touched on the government’s intention to involve persons with disabilities in its governance. He cautioned the beneficiaries against selling of their items for physical cash. The Hon. DCE closing his speech encouraged persons with disabilities to be entrepreneurial in their day to day activities in order to keep up with the pace of development in the district and in the country as a whole. Mr. Augustine Atoa Gyabaa, the chairman of the District's PWDs thanked the president, Nana Addo Danquah Akuffo Addo and the District Chief Executive for their immense support to persons with disabilities. He also tasked those receiving the items to make good use of them .He again urged beneficiaries to engage themselves in businesses that will generate income to help improve their livelihoods. ~Charles Amponssh (0245100257)


HON. OPPONG KWADWO FRANCIS , THE DISTRICT CHIEF EXECUTIVE FOR DORMAA WEST OBSERVES THE 65TH INDEPENDENCE DAY CELEBRATION AT NKRANKWANTA

2022

The District Chief Executive for Dormaa West , Hon.Francis Kwadwo Oppong , today , March 6, 2022 , on behalf of the President of the republic of Ghana , Nana Addo Dankwa Akufo -Addo observed the 65th independence day celebration at Nkrankwanta , the capital of Dormaa West District. In attendance were Nana Agyei Akyea Akombi II , the chief of Nkrankwanta and his elders , the Former MP for Dormaa West , Hon. Ali Maiga Halidu , Assembly members , Heads of Security agencies , Heads of Departments , Political parties reps , the clergy, the member of Parliament and the Muslim community . In delivering his speech , on the theme " Working together , bouncing back Better" , Hon. Francis Kwadwo Oppong charged the people to unite patriotically and contribute their quota in diverse ways in order to timely revamp the economy that was tempered by the COVID-19 pandemic , for it took the social , political and the economic struggle of "The big six " and some ex-service men to redeem Ghana from the colonial suppression . He prompted the people that , one of the measures that can bring Ghana's economy back on track in this hard times is the honest fulfillment of our tax obligations as citizens and clear example of the Electronic Transaction Levy ( E- Levy ) intended to be introduced by the government . The DCE therefore pleaded with the people to accept the passage of the tax for a better and a grounded nation building . He entreated the people to utilize the afforestation and the Green Ghana policies , formulated and implemented by the Nana Addo's government . He stated categorically that , the laws frown at illegal Lumbering and logging, has caused Ghana a lot because deserted lands pave ways for heavy wind storms , prevent periodic rains and depletes the ozone layer . He finally sympathized and comforted the victimized schools and homes of the Thursday's rain storm at Nkrankwanta and assured them of government’s intervention.

Mast for rural telecommunication network at Adiemmra / Nzezera, a community in the Asuopiri West Electoral area of the Dormaa West District.

2022

Today, Monday, 16th May, 2022, the DCE for Dormaa West District, Hon. Francis Kwadwo Oppong together with some assembly staff led by the Coordinating director, Mr Idrissu Mahama were joined by officers from Ghana Investment For Electronic Communication(GIFEC) to look for siting to erect a mast for rural telecommunication network at Adiemmra / Nzezera, a community in the Asuopiri West Electoral area of the Dormaa West District. Mr. Akowua Terrence, Manager at GIFEC, said the government in it's quest to ensure that rural areas in Ghana have access to telephone network is putting up over 2000 rural telephony networks which Adiemmra/ Nzezera is not left out. He said the project will start soon and will be completed in due course. The Obaahemaa of the Community thanked the DCE and the government for the intervention. She offered a piece of land to be used for the project. The DCE was accompanied by some polling station executive and the Electoral area Coordinator, Mr. Alex Agyekum. ~Charles Amponsah. (P.A)

HON. AFRIYIE AKOTO (AGRIC MINISTER) AND THE BONO REGIONAL MINISTER VISIT NKRANKWANTA.

2022

Yesterday, Tuesday, June 14, 2022, the Minister for Agriculture, Hon. Owusu Afriyie Akoto and the Bono Regional Minister, Hon. Justina Awo Banahene paid a working visit to Nkrankwanta, the capital of Dormaa West District. The minister for Agriculture and the Bono regional Minister inspected the Nkrankwanta Rice processing factory under the ( 1D1F ) policy. He also met with the Dormaa West Rice Farmers Association where he officially inaugurated the association and charged them with the responsibility of farming and providing enough raw material to feed the factory. The minister assured them of the government's support, being it Extension services, seed, fertilizer, herbicides, and machinery. Speaking on behalf of the Nkrankwanta Rice Processing Company, the secretary and board member, Dr. Aziz Khalid thanked the minister and the government for the setting up of the facility in the Dormaa West District. He said the vacant lands that remained in the district are not favorable for the cultivation of any other crop, other than rice, coconut, and oil palm. He said they are ever ready to contribute their quota in visualizing and materializing the government’s policy of " Planting for Food and Jobs " if only the government gives them a helping hand in providing them with both human, capital, and material resources. They were accompanied by the Dormaa West District Chief Executive, Hon. Francis Kwadwo Oppong, the District Assembly staff, and NPP party executives.


Hon. Francis Kwadwo Oppong honoured an invitation to attend a meeting organised by the Dormas West District Cocoa Health Extension Division (CHED) at the methodist Church, Nkrankwanta.

2022

Yesterday, Wednesday, 15th June, 2022, the District Chief Executive for Dormaa West District, Hon. Francis Kwadwo Oppong honoured an invitation to attend a meeting organised by the Dormas West District Cocoa Health Extension Division (CHED) at the methodist Church, Nkrankwanta. Speaking at the meeting, Hon. Francis Kwadwo Oppong reiterated government’s plan to support farmers to improve on their farm products. He said cocoa farmers should embrace the government’s Cocoa rehabilitation programme. He said the rehabilitation programme will help farmers to regain their lost cocoa farms. The DCE cautioned farmers, Cocoa mass sprayers and the gang leaders against selling of farm inputs which have been subsidised by the government. He urged the farmers to always form associations and or cooperatives to help CHED distribute farm inputs to them equitably. He said his office is open to all and sundry so farmers and CHED workers could pass by anytime the need arises. Mr. David Afriyie Gyebi, District Director (CHED) thanked Hon. Francis Kwadwo Oppong for honouring their invitation.
